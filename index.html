Episode Guide

S1E1: The Evil That Lies Within, Part 1

Air Date: 1986-09-21

A Soviet drilling project accidentally breaches the underground realm of the Inhumanoids, releasing the monstrous D'Compose and his undead minions. Earth Corps, a specialized team of scientists and soldiers, must investigate the threat while dealing with the corporate interests of Blackthorne Shore who seeks to exploit the discovery.

S1E2: The Evil That Lies Within, Part 2

Air Date: 1986-09-28

The Inhumanoids continue their assault on the surface world. Earth Corps discovers the existence of Metlar, the most powerful of the evil Inhumanoids, while trying to understand the ancient conflict between the monsters of the underground.

S1E3: The Evil That Lies Within, Part 3

Air Date: 1986-10-05

Earth Corps allies with the Mutores, benevolent underground beings led by Magnakor, to fight against the Inhumanoids. The team learns about the ancient war between good and evil forces beneath the Earth's surface.

S1E4: The Evil That Lies Within, Part 4

Air Date: 1986-10-12

The battle intensifies as Metlar seeks to break free from his ancient prison. Earth Corps must work with their new Mutore allies to prevent the most dangerous Inhumanoid from reaching the surface and destroying humanity.

S1E5: The Evil That Lies Within, Part 5

Air Date: 1986-10-19

In the climactic conclusion of the opening arc, Earth Corps and the Mutores make their final stand against the Inhumanoids. The fate of both the surface and underground worlds hangs in the balance.

S1E6: Coup de Graa

Air Date: 1986-10-26

Earth Corps splits up to tackle multiple threats - some heading to Skellweb to save Sandra from D'Compose's touch, while others venture into Infernac where Blackthorne faces off against Metlar.

S1E7: Nightstalker

Air Date: 1986-11-02

When Nightcrawler is injured, Earth Corps must find a way to heal their Mutore ally while defending against a new Inhumanoid threat that emerges from the depths.

S1E8: The Masterson Team

Air Date: 1986-11-09

A rival team led by Derek Masterson challenges Earth Corps' methods and authority in dealing with the Inhumanoid crisis, creating dangerous complications.

S1E9: Contest of Champions

Air Date: 1986-11-16

The forces of good and evil engage in a supernatural contest that will determine the fate of both the surface world and the underground realm.

S1E10: Siren's Song

Air Date: 1986-11-23

A mysterious song with hypnotic powers threatens to turn Earth Corps against each other while the Inhumanoids plot their next devastating attack.

S1E11: Metamorphosis

Air Date: 1986-11-30

Shocking transformations occur as the line between friend and foe becomes blurred, and Earth Corps must face the possibility that one of their own may be lost forever.

S1E12: The Surma Plan

Air Date: 1986-12-07

Blackthorne's ultimate scheme is revealed as he attempts to harness the power of the Inhumanoids for his own corporate empire, putting all of humanity at risk.

S1E13: Auger... for Hire

Air Date: 1986-12-14

When Auger is captured and brainwashed by the enemy, Earth Corps must find a way to save their teammate while preventing him from being used as a weapon against them.

Comics

The Coming of the Inhumanoids: Part 1 of 4

Based on the screenplay by Flint Dille. Adapted by Jim Salicrup. Art and cover by James W. Fry and Joe Del Beato. Inhumanoids is 1-part 1950s monster movie, 1-part sci-fi adventure, and 1-part ecological warning. A group of scientists, strapped inside high tech suits of armor, explore the catacombs of the earth and discover that ancient evil creatures called the Inhumanoids lay in wait to rise up and conqueror mankind. 32 pages Cover price $0.75.

The Coming of the Inhumanoids: Part 2 of 4

"I Left My Monsters in San Francisco!" Based on the screenplay by Flint Dille. Adapted by Jim Salicrup. Art and cover by James W. Fry and Joe Del Beato. The Inhumanoids, monstrous behemoths of immense power, live again! What can the monsters, Tendril and D'Compose possibly want? The scientists of Earth Corps soon found themselves caught in the middle of a centuries-old war between these Inhumanoids and the Mutores, benevolent elemental creatures of rock, wood, fire, and ice. 32 pages Cover price $0.75.

The Coming of the Inhumanoids: Part 3 of 4

"The Battle Down Below!" Based on the screenplay by Flint Dille. Adapted by Jim Salicrup. Art by Jose Delbo and Art Thibert. Armed with new weapons and a plan, the Earth Corps venture down into the bowels of the earth to confront the dangerous Inhumanoids. But are they ready for what they find when they enter the domain of D'Compose? 32 pages Cover price $0.75.

The Coming of the Inhumanoids: Part 4 of 4

"Metlar Unleashed!" Based on the screenplay by Flint Dille. Adapted by Jim Salicrup. Art by Carmine Infantino and Danny Bulanadi. How can the brave members of Earth Corps and the Mutores hope to triumph over the evil Inhumanoids after the unstoppable Metlar is unleashed?! FC, 32 pages NOTE: Unfortunately this series ends on a cliffhanger and the resolution to this story has never seen print. Cover price $1.00.
